Abstract

Scalar field dark matter likely is able to solve the small-scale cosmology problems facing the cold dark matter (CDM), and has become an emerging contender to challenge the CDM. It however requires a particle mass ∼1−2×10−22similar-toabsent12superscript1022\sim 1-2\times 10^{-22}eV. We find such an extremely small particle mass can naturally arise from a non-QCD axion mechanism, under fairly general assumptions that the axion is the dominant dark matter, and a few species of self-interacting light particles of comparable masses and a massless gauge boson both decouple from the bright sector since the photon temperature exceeds 200 GeV. These assumptions also set the axion decay constant scale to several ×1016absentsuperscript1016\times 10^{16} GeV. Given the above axion mass, we further pin down the dark-sector particles to consist of single-handed fermion and anti-fermion. With a mass around 92−1289212892-128 eV, these dark-sector fermions may constitute a minority population of dark matter. In the simplest SU(2) gauge field model, a dilute instanton gas as dark photons can contribute to about 2.5%percent2.52.5\% of the total relativistic relics in the cosmic microwave background radiation.

I Introduction

The scalar field dark matter has been in recent years gaining supports as a viable dark matter candidate hu (4, 3, 1, 2, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9, 10). In particular, the complex scalar field, dubbed ψ𝜓\psiDM, of boson mass ma=10−22subscript𝑚𝑎superscript1022m_{a}=10^{-22}eV has been shown to solve the most pressing small-scale cosmology problems, i.e., the core-cusp problem and the missing satellite problem of CDM sch1 (1). It also solves the catch-22 problem facing the warm dark matter mac (11). These are all small-scale problems associated with small galaxies. Observations of Lyman-α𝛼\alpha forests place another constraint on the next scale about 111Mpc (co-moving coordinate) for alternative dark matter models sel (12, 13). ψ𝜓\psiDM of ma≈10−22subscript𝑚𝑎superscript1022m_{a}\approx 10^{-22}eV has been shown to be marginally consistent with the data of Lyman-α𝛼\alpha forests mar (10), but an increase of particle mass by a factor 2 will definitely ease the tension.

From astrophysics standpoint, ψ𝜓\psiDM offers a new window of opportunity for quantitative comparisons with more unsolved problems. But from particle physics standpoint, it remains enigmatic for a particle that is so light. String theories have predicted an axiverse with a hierarchy of light particle masses asi (14, 15). The axiverse has so far gained no support from astronomical observations, which tend to favor a narrow range of mass scale. Despite this, the axion mechanism is still very attractive, in that it has almost no tunable parameter in some regime, thus making the predictions solid and definitive.

Axion mechanism is originally proposed by Peccei and Quinn to solve the CP problem in strong interactions pec1 (16, 17). III Dark axion model

Let us suppose the dark sector to consist of weakly-interacting fermions with a mixture of Dirac and Majorana masses so that they contain a finite CP violating phase ϕitalic-ϕ\phi, and the massless S​U​(2)𝑆𝑈2SU(2) gauge bosons that form instantons and gauge couple to fermions. The instanton ground state is a superposition of a series of individual instantons of different positive and negative integer winding numbers, i.e., a dilute instanton gas. When fermions annihilate, the CP violating phase ϕitalic-ϕ\phi must go somewhere. The instanton gas would need to adjust to generate an energetically unfavorable fractional averaged winding number to accommodate the arbitrary CP violating ϕitalic-ϕ\phi. To ease the tension, a third dynamical field θ¯¯𝜃\bar{\theta} therefore arises and in place of instantons to absorb ϕitalic-ϕ\phi. With the presence of θ¯¯𝜃\bar{\theta}, the instantons have an average winding number ∝θ¯+ϕproportional-toabsent¯𝜃italic-ϕ\propto\bar{\theta}+\phi, which is energetically favorable since ⟨(θ¯+ϕ)⟩=0delimited-⟨⟩¯𝜃italic-ϕ0\langle(\bar{\theta}+\phi)\rangle=0. Here, θ¯+ϕ(≡θ)annotated¯𝜃italic-ϕabsent𝜃\bar{\theta}+\phi(\equiv\theta) is the axion field and proportional to the force acting on the axion; hence the axion potential is ∝θ2proportional-toabsentsuperscript𝜃2\propto\theta^{2}.

When one further identifies θ𝜃\theta to be the angle of the large field, one immediately realizes that it is the CP violating phase ϕitalic-ϕ\phi of dark-sector fermions that breaks the rotational symmetry and induces a tilt to the originally axisymmetric potential of the large field. Thus, the action of transferring ϕitalic-ϕ\phi is in between the dark-sector fermions and the axions; the instanton gas is merely a background medium that is passively perturbed by the release of ϕitalic-ϕ\phi. Hence when CP violating fermions annihilate, the released rest mass energy must largely be converted to the axion potential to produce resonance oscillation, and leaves little to share with the instanton random excitations as heat.111Although the underlying axion scenario is similar to QCD, the present model is in great contrast to QCD. Instantons in QCD are tightly coupled to quarks, and when the latter are annihilated the former will no longer exist. But in the present model with weak interactions, an almost free instanton gas is still present as dark photons even in the absence of dark fermions.

The above picture is in the zero temperature limit. At finite temperature, the picture does not change, except one must distinguish the rest mass energy that is converted to the axion potential and the kinetic energy that is dumped to the heat bath. The rest mass energy is released at the time when particles become trans-relativistic, and we have indeed seen Λ∼T¯msimilar-toΛsubscript¯𝑇𝑚\Lambda\sim\bar{T}_{m} for a wide range of r​gd𝑟subscript𝑔𝑑rg_{d}, which is strongly suggestive that dark particles have masses ∼Λsimilar-toabsentΛ\sim\Lambda and they are just becoming trans-relativistic to make the energy conversion.
