Abstract

Denote by qn​(G)subscript𝑞𝑛𝐺q_{n}(G) the smallest eigenvalue of the signless Laplacian matrix of an n𝑛n-vertex graph G𝐺G. Brandt conjectured in 1997 that for regular triangle-free graphs qn​(G)≤4​n25subscript𝑞𝑛𝐺4𝑛25q_{n}(G)\leq\frac{4n}{25}. We prove a stronger result: If G𝐺G is a triangle-free graph then qn​(G)≤15​n94<4​n25subscript𝑞𝑛𝐺15𝑛944𝑛25q_{n}(G)\leq\frac{15n}{94}<\frac{4n}{25}. Brandt’s conjecture is a subproblem of two famous conjectures of Erdős:

(1) Sparse-Half-Conjecture: Every n𝑛n-vertex triangle-free graph has a subset of vertices of size ⌈n2⌉𝑛2\lceil\frac{n}{2}\rceil spanning at most n2/50superscript𝑛250n^{2}/50 edges.

(2) Every n𝑛n-vertex triangle-free graph can be made bipartite by removing at most n2/25superscript𝑛225n^{2}/25 edges.

In our proof we use linear algebraic methods to upper bound qn​(G)subscript𝑞𝑛𝐺q_{n}(G) by the ratio between the number of induced paths with 3 and 4 vertices. We give an upper bound on this ratio via the method of flag algebras.

1 Introduction

We prove a result on eigenvalues of triangle-free graphs which is motivated by the following two famous conjectures of Erdős.

Conjecture 1.1 (Erdős’ Sparse Half Conjecture [9, 10]).

Every triangle-free graph on n𝑛n vertices has a subset of vertices of size ⌈n2⌉𝑛2\lceil\frac{n}{2}\rceil vertices spanning at most n2/50superscript𝑛250n^{2}/50 edges.

Erdős offered a $250 reward for proving this conjecture. There has been progress on this conjecture in various directions [12, 14, 17, 15, 4]. Most recently, Razborov [17] proved that every triangle-free graph on n𝑛n vertices has an induced subgraph on n/2𝑛2n/2 vertices with at most (27/1024)​n2271024superscript𝑛2(27/1024)n^{2} edges.
